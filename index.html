Overview of recent technological advances based on AI/ML methods in the context of irrigation water management

Abstract

This paper seeks to explore the efficacy of using artificial intelligence techniques for optimizing irrigation methods within the framework of Agriculture 4.0. Therefore, in this context, we propose a comprehensive review of existing research on irrigation optimization systems based on artificial intelligence. In addition to this related work on the subject, we present the most relevant AI-based methods used in this field. For the analysis, we used specific keywords with a co-occurrence-based network technique. The main results of this analysis, presented below, show the following words ranked in order of frequency: “machine learning”, “artificial intelligence”, “precision agriculture”, “agriculture”, “deep learning”, “irrigation”, “IoT”, “random forest”, “LSTM” and “sensors”. Finally, an overview of water management in gravity-fed irrigation systems is given, with an emphasis on decision support systems for irrigation scheduling is presented with focus on the solutions from Côte d’Ivoire.

Method

Dataset search

Irrigation efficiency is essential for irrigated rice growing, as it maximizes the productivity of the water used, which is crucial in a context of limited water resources. Current irrigation methods can be inefficient, particularly in terms of water transport and application. These inefficiencies can lead to significant water losses and sub-optimal use of resources. To overcome these limitations, it is proposed to adopt advanced technologies and improved management practices. For example, the use of variable-volume irrigation systems and sensors for precise irrigation control can improve overall efficiency. Studies have shown that overall irrigation efficiency can be improved by optimizing water transport (ET) and application efficiency (EA). These data are essential for developing more efficient irrigation strategies. State-of-the-art irrigation methods have been tested to assess their effectiveness. For example, the use of drones to monitor fields and adjust irrigation in real time may offer viable solutions for improving irrigation efficiency.This review would summarize the latest technological advances and research in this field :

To distinguish relevant papers on the topic, electronic scientific databases such as ArXiv and Scopus and indexing services such as Google Scholar and ResearchGate were searched on June 13, 2024 for papers on irrigation efficiency and artificial intelligence. Preference was given to articles produced in the last five (5) years on the subject. This represents a total of 1,607 scientific articles per continent. These articles were identified on the basis of combined keywords such as: {Artificial intelligence} + {Agriculture} or {Artificial intelligence} + {Precision agriculture}; {Irrigation efficiency} + {Optimization} or {Irrigation efficiency} + {Water management}.
